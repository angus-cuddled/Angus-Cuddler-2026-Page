By the fire

Winter warmer cocktails

Cosy, spirit-forward drinks for cold nights on the range. Most are single-serve — see the batch notes to scale up. Mix mild, taste, then adjust.

Hot & served warm

Hot Toddy (whisky)

You’ll need: 60ml whisky; 2 tsp honey; 15ml lemon juice; ~150ml hot water; lemon wheel, cinnamon stick, cloves.

Method: Warm a heatproof mug with hot water, then empty. Add honey, lemon juice & whisky, top with hot (not boiling) water, stir to dissolve the honey. Garnish with a clove-studded lemon wheel & a cinnamon stick.

The classic cold-night cure. Batch: warm whisky + honey + lemon gently in a pot (don’t boil) and ladle into mugs.

Hot Buttered Bourbon

You’ll need: 60ml bourbon; 1 tbsp ‘batter’ (softened butter creamed with brown sugar + pinch cinnamon, nutmeg, clove); hot water.

Method: Spoon the spiced butter batter into a mug, add bourbon, top with hot water and stir until the butter melts into a silky, sweet drink.

Batch: make a tub of the butter batter ahead (butter + brown sugar + mixed spice). Everyone just spoons & tops up.

Spiked Mexican Hot Chocolate

You’ll need: 45ml reposado tequila (or bourbon); 250ml hot chocolate; pinch cinnamon & chilli; whipped cream.

Method: Make rich hot chocolate on the stove, whisk in a pinch of cinnamon and a tiny pinch of chilli. Off the heat, stir in the tequila. Top with cream and a dusting of cinnamon.

Crowd favourite & kid-friendly minus the booze — make a big pot of the choc, then spike the adults’ mugs individually.

Mulled-style Bourbon Cider

You’ll need: 60ml bourbon; 200ml cloudy apple juice/cider; 1 cinnamon stick, 2 cloves, orange peel; star anise.

Method: Gently warm the juice/cider with the spices & orange peel for 10 min (don’t boil). Pour into mugs and add a nip of bourbon to each.

Brilliant for a group: simmer a big pot of spiced cider on the stove, ladle out and add bourbon per mug.

Stirred & spirit-forward

Old Fashioned (bourbon/whisky)

You’ll need: 60ml bourbon or rye; 1 sugar cube (or 7ml sugar syrup); 2–3 dashes Angostura bitters; orange peel.

Method: Muddle the sugar with bitters & a splash of water in a glass. Add a big ice cube and the whisky, stir 20–30 sec until chilled. Express an orange peel over the top and drop it in.

The benchmark winter sipper. Batch: mix whisky, syrup, bitters & a little water in a bottle, chill, pour over ice.

Whisky / Bourbon Sour

You’ll need: 60ml bourbon; 30ml lemon juice; 20ml sugar syrup; 1 egg white (optional); bitters.

Method: Shake everything hard with ice (dry-shake first without ice if using egg white for foam). Strain over fresh ice. Dot bitters on the foam.

Egg white optional but gives a lovely foam. Pre-mix the bourbon + lemon + syrup base by the jug, shake to order.

Tequila Añejo Old Fashioned

You’ll need: 60ml añejo tequila; 7ml agave syrup; 2 dashes orange (or Angostura) bitters; orange peel.

Method: Stir tequila, agave and bitters over a large ice cube until well chilled. Express orange peel over and garnish.

A warming, oaky twist on the Old Fashioned for the tequila drinkers. Use a good aged (añejo) tequila.

Maple Whisky Sour / Manhattan-ish

You’ll need: 60ml rye or bourbon; 25ml sweet vermouth (Manhattan) OR 20ml maple syrup + 25ml lemon (maple sour); bitters; cherry.

Method: Manhattan — stir whisky, vermouth & 2 dashes bitters over ice, strain into a chilled glass, cherry. Maple sour — shake whisky, maple & lemon with ice, strain over fresh ice.

Two easy riffs on one bottle of whisky. Maple syrup makes a lovely wintry sweetener.

📝 Handy notes

  • Buy: a bottle each of bourbon & whisky, one añejo/reposado tequila, Angostura bitters, honey, maple syrup, lemons, oranges, cinnamon sticks, cloves, star anise, sweet vermouth, cloudy apple juice/cider, good drinking chocolate & cream.
  • Hot drinks: warm gently, never boil — boiling cooks off the alcohol and turns citrus bitter.
  • Kid-friendly: the hot chocolate and spiced cider both work beautifully without the spirit — spike the adults’ mugs only.
  • Designated driver / nightcap: these are strong. Keep water on the table and go easy if anyone’s driving down the range.
